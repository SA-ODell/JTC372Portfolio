whatIDo

An aspiring full-stack web and iOS developer, I'm currently doing things a little backwards: after six months of web development bootcamp, now I'm back in school as a senior citizen college freshman. I am currently enrolled in Colorado State University's computer science program, enjoying ever minute of life in Fort Collins.

howIGotHere

After five years of foster parenting, I decided to build a mobile app to help foster parents securely organize their kids' information, appointments, and reporting requirements. I started with Objective-C, then dabbled in Swift before going back to the basics with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. That led to an opportunity to learn full-stack web development at the Galvanize campus in Denver. After that, I leveraged my remaining GI Bill benefits to move to Fort Collins, where I'm a full-time computer science student, and diving back into mobile app development on the side.

whyILoveIt

As an Army engineer, I loved the never-ending challenges of solving new problems and also old problems in a new environment. Web development is all of that, with a little extra mustard. It's contributing to open source projects, trying to make a difference in big ways and small, and using the ultimate communication platform to help everyone move forward. And maybe most importantly, web development is never standing still, always learning, and always improving on the last go-round.
